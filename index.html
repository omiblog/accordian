FAQ

What is a monoclonal antibody?

Antibodies are proteins that people’s bodies make to fight viruses such as COVID-19. Monoclonal Antibodies are antibodies made in a laboratory that act as natural antibodies to limit the number of viruses in your body.

How does it work?

Monoclonal Antibodies are given into a vein by intravenous (iv) infusion. Antibodies may be administered only in settings where healthcare providers have immediate access to medications to treat any reactions, like Memorial Village ER.

When should I get Monoclonal Antibody infusion?

If you have tested positive for COVID-19 or have COVID-19 symptoms such as cough, sneezing/ runny nose, body aches, fever, headache, abdominal discomfort, and sore throat.
